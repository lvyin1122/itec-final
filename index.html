What is Anxiety

Anxiety is a subjective feeling of unhappiness, uneasiness and discomfort. It is an emotion characterized by an unpleasant state of inner confusion. And it is usually a kind of generalized, unfocused and overreaction to a certain situation.

Sometimes, people feel mental anxiety, while some people's anxiety will be accompanied by a certain degree of physical discomfort. According to KY survey results(2019), the more serious the anxiety, the higher the level of physical discomfort is, and there is a positive correlation between them.

What affects the level of anxiety?

Genetic

Previous research have confirmed that anxiety sensitivity, as well as some physiological factors related to anxiety, are genetically related. And the heritability of these anxiety related factors in women is greater than that in men.

Women are more likely to cause anxiety due to their higher degree of neuroticism than men, such as sexual assault.The shaping process of gender roles makes women more sensitive to social rules and norms. However, the stereotype of men in society will also lead to more pressure on men in career and anxiety.

Age

Age can affect people's anxiety levels. The older the people, the lower their anxiety level. This may be due to the fact that with the increase of age, people's life is more regular than when they were young, and the frequency of adverse life events is reduced; at the same time, career development reduces the economic pressure; while people enter a later stage of life, to some extent, it also reduces the negative emotions caused by parents' marriage problems (Henderson, jorm , Korten,Jacomb, Christensen & Rodgers，1998）。

Social Connection

Higher the frequency of exercise, the higher the frequency of social interaction, less the time of using the Internet will lead to lower anxiety levels. Among the people who exercise every day, only 20.96% of them have a high level of objective anxiety, while among the people who never exercise, this proportion is almost twice that of the former, accounting for 39.56%; similarly, among the people who have social activities every day, 23.59% have a high level of objective anxiety, while 39.11% of the people who never participate in social activities have a high level of anxiety.

Major Causes for Anxiety

About the cause of anxiety, nearly a quarter (24.7 percent) of people are anxious because their academic or career performance cannot meet expectations, while 16.78 percent people’s stress is the result of unsatisfactory intimate relationship or not having it at all. Another 15.77 percent people’s stress comes from economic pressures.

To summarize, anxiety could be avoided only if people achieve personal accomplishments, receive love and wealth simultaneously.

Marriage Status Makes a Difference in Anxiety Reasons

Gender makes little difference regarding the major causes of anxiety, but marriage status does.

Unmarried people are mostly worried about themselves, and academic / career development bothers more than a quarter of single people. However, people with marriage experience (married, divorced or widowed) tend to concentrate more on problems related to their children though they might also concern about their career. Nearly a tenth of them are constantly worried about their inability to raise and educate their children well.

Uncertainty: The Major Source of Anxiety

Uncertainty is considered to be the major source of anxiety. The more and more widespread anxiety of modern people is not only related to the degree of uncertainty that each of us can feel, but also related to individual's tolerance of uncertainty. The tolerance for uncertainty measures the extent to which people need and look for predictability and how they react in uncertain situations (Eysenck, derakshan, Santos & Calvo, 2007).

Researchers have invited interviewees to judge five statements, such as "unexpected things will make me very depressed", "even the smallest doubt will stop me from doing something", and measured everyone's tolerance for uncertainty accordingly; interviewees also rated the level of uncertainty they felt (from a scale of 1-10 points). The results of the data show that the lower people's tolerance for uncertainty, the more uncertainty they feel about life, followed by a higher anxiety level.




Why causes of young people's anxiety become abstract?

Compared to the past, the causes of young people's anxiety are more abstract nowadays, not only because of specific problems like the daily upsetting job or the difficulty of receiving medical treatment. Apart from reasons such as the inability to realize self-worth or having no sense of existence, many people in the survey responded that "anxiety comes from none of the above reasons, and I just can’t figure out why".

Increasing Individual’s Mobility

This kind of anxiety, which is hard to trace to specific reasons, not only roots from personal psychology, but also arises from the impact of structural changes in the society as a whole. In the past three decades, China's social structure has changed dramatically, and individual’s mobility has increased. Many individuals left their original groups and families to enter new cities. In this process, most people have experienced a certain degree of "individualization" and “fragmentation”. The experience across generations has become somewhat useless, and social connections have been reduced (Twenge, 2000).

Effects of Anxiety

According to our survey, more than half of the respondents (53.57%) confirmed that anxiety has a positive effect. In fact, an appropriate level of anxiety can indeed help people objectively understand their situation. In this survey, 27.31%, 22.31%, and 18.24% of the respondents believe that anxiety can “provide an opportunity to reflect on life, help to better understand themselves”, “strengthen the motivation to study / work”, and “enhance attention towards life risk".


On the other hand, anxiety can really affect the performance of tasks that require more concentration or short-term memory (Eysenck & Calvo, 1992). In the survey, respondents considered that the three most important negative effects caused by anxiety were “declining attention, poor learning / work efficiency”, “avoiding social interaction”, “declining appetite or poor sleep”, accounting for 23.84% of all responses, 20.27%, 16.86%.


The impact of anxiety varies. However, 83.07% of the respondents said that they have tried to actively improve their anxiety. We have also collected some opinions and methods on how to coexist with anxiety: 26.09% and 47.36% respectively I think that finding a suitable lifestyle can help or relieve anxiety greatly, and learning meditation / participating in regular exercise and finding good friends to communicate can also help reduce anxiety.


More than half of the respondents think that addicting to games / Internet is the most ineffective way. 31.24% and 28.33% of people believe that eating help is very small or relatively small, and even seemingly positive behaviors such as hard work is essentially avoiding anxiety, may have "miracle effects" in the short term, but aggravate anxiety in the long term.

How to beat Anxiety


1. Focus on things that controllable.

Since anxiety is largely a result of people's uncertain feelings, you can try to turn expectations into plans. When facing big challenges, instead of worrying about possible failures and restless, try to write down the reasons why they may cause failures, and find out if there is a way to avoid these failures. If there is really a risk that you can't do, then you can plan how to deal with this risk.

At the same time, try to start with the things you can control, dismantle the goal, and gradually find a sense of mastery. For example, the long-term goal is to complete a doctoral dissertation. The small task that can be refined to every day is to write 500 words in half a day. Sometimes you can also give yourself some small rewards, such as completing 5000 words to reward yourself for buying a small jewelry.

Many people's anxiety is widespread, and it is easy to think negatively about things, and that can be consciously trained to change cognitive biases. Some psychologists will advise people to pay attention to and record the five pleasant things they encounter every day, hoping to gradually shift their attention to the positive aspects of things.

2. Observe anxiety and embody your feelings

Some people may be trapped by the anxiety itself, so what you have to do is:

Become an observer of your own feelings, because there may be deeper emotions behind some anxiety, and anxiety is only a means of defense for people. If many ways to relieve anxiety are not working for you, identifying the true source behind them is key.

For example, when you worry about "I will be lonely", you may be thinking, " I eat too much, my habits are bad, and no one will like me." When you capture and observe your feelings, you may find that it is not that uncertain future that bothers you, but "I want to escape these negative feelings."

You can also try to label idea categories instead of focusing on the content of the idea. When you notice that you are worried (such as when you start worrying that you will fail or experience a loss), label it "I'm worried". If you start criticizing yourself, label it "criticism." This process can help you get rid of the content of the idea, avoid being trapped by the content, and make you more aware of the process of "what am I doing" and "why do I have these ideas", so that you may be able to realize, There are other more objective and peaceful ways to look at the situation now.

Don't be afraid of anxiety itself. Believe that you can talk to, live with, and even overcome or take advantage of this emotion.

3. Learn to relax, do not escape

You can choose to meditate, exercise, chat with friends, or even do things that are not related to your current goals, such as doing laundry. These things can help you increase your sense of control and keep you from getting stuck in anxiety. Many interviewees mentioned in their stories that meditation and exercise really help them get rid of their anxiety and focus more on the tasks that should be done now. You can choose some meditation tools, improve your concentration, or improve your sleep quality, which can help improve your ability to control your emotions.

It can be seen from the survey that our perception of our anxiety is sometimes not so accurate. Looking at the sharing in the circle of friends and listening to others constantly talking about anxiety, we unconsciously exaggerated the anxiety that was normalized. It is true that anxiety often comes from uncertainty, but the uncertainty of life is a normal state of life and it also means more possibilities.

I hope we can all accept the changes and ambiguities in life with a calmer attitude. Don't be afraid, walk in with a more positive attitude, and try to use your own strength to explore your own path out of this mist.

If you are worried that you will feel powerless in the face of this anxious era, then look for more interpersonal connections, talk with friends about your true thoughts, and try to talk to your family about your current situation and feelings.

Anxiety is often not the problem itself. It is more like a signpost, trying to understand the source of anxiety, maybe it can reveal some of our own secrets and potentials to us. In an age of constant talk about anxiety and selling anxiety, you may wish to be a person who accepts and understands anxiety.
